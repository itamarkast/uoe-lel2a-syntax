        \item No subject requirement in nominals (no expletive subjects needed).
    \end{itemize}

\frame{\frametitle{Interim summary}
    Technical implementation:
        \begin{enumerate}
            \item Definite/indefinite article in \obar{D}.
            \item Possessive pronouns in \obar{D}.
            \item Possessor noun (DP) in Spec,DP.
            \item Argument of the nominal: complement to \obar{N}.
            \item Modifier of the nominal: adjoined to \ibar{N}.
        \end{enumerate}

    \bigskip 
    \ding{228} The formalism conveys broader generalisations (e.g.~complementary distribution of \emph{the} and \emph{my}).

    \bigskip
     Argument or modifier of N?
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Test 1: argument of the underlying verb.
        \item Test 2: conjunction, etc (as with verbs).
        \item Special case: complement vs relative clauses.
    \end{enumerate}
}
